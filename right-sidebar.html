This is some of the Merit based Scholarships

CRITERIA :

  • Academic Performance: Strong academic records, usually with a specified minimum percentage or grade point average (GPA).
  • Entrance Exam Scores: High scores in competitive exams like JEE, NEET, or other national/state-level exams.
  • Rank in Competitive Exams: Top rankings or performance in relevant competitive exams within a particular field.
  • Special Achievements: Recognition in sports, arts, cultural activities, or other extracurricular areas.
  • Financial Background: Some scholarships may consider the applicant's financial background.
  • Recommendations: Strong letters of recommendation from teachers or mentors.
  • Essays or Interviews: Written essays or interviews assessing motivation and goals.
  • Specific Criteria: Some scholarships have specific requirements related to course of study, location, gender, or minority status.
  • Research or Projects: Participation or achievement in research projects or innovative initiatives.
  • Leadership Roles: Holding leadership positions in school, college, or community organizations.

      ITC Fee Reimbursement Scholarship, Kerala

      Provider – Department of Minority Welfare, Government of Kerala Eligibility – • Open for students who belong to Minority communities (Muslim, Christian, Parsi, Sikh, Buddhist, and Jains). • The applicants must be pursuing an ITI course. • The annual income of the family should be less than INR 8,00,000 per annum. Awards • INR 10,000 for students pursuing the 1-year course. • INR 20,000 for students pursuing the 2-year course. Application Period* – Between November and December Application Process – Applicants can apply online through Minority Welfare Scholarships Portal.

      C H Muhammedkoya Scholarship, Kerala

      Provider – Department of Minority Welfare, Government of Kerala Eligibility – • Open for girl students only who belong to Muslim, Latin, or converted Christian communities. • The applicants must be pursuing studies at graduation level or above. • The percentage of marks to be obtained by them in the last qualifying examination should be 50% or more. • The annual income of the family should be less than INR 8,00,000. • Students studying in self-financing colleges from merit seats can also apply. • Awards – • For undergraduates – INR 5,000 per annum • For postgraduates – INR 6,000 per annum • For professional courses – INR 7,000 per annum • Hostel stipend – INR 13,000 per annum

      APJ Abdul Kalam Scholarship (APJAK), Kerala

      Provider – Department of Minority Welfare, Government of Kerala Eligibility – • Students belonging to Minority communities (Muslims, Sikhs, Christians, Parsis, Jains, and Buddhists) can apply for this scholarship. • They must be studying in a government/aided polytechnic in a merit seat. • The annual income of the family should be less than INR 8,00,000. Award – INR 6,000 per annum Application Period*- Between August and November Application Process – Applicants can apply online through Minority Welfare Scholarships Portal.

      Prof. Joseph Mundassery Scholarship Award (PJMS), Kerala

      Provider – Department of Minority Welfare, Government of Kerala Eligibility – • Applicants belonging to the Minority community (Muslims, Christians, Sikhs, Parsis, Jains, and Buddhists) can apply for this scholarship. • They must be pursuing their education in Classes 10, 12, and VHSE. • The annual income of the family should be less than INR 8,00,000 from all sources. Award – INR 10,000 for each selected scholar at different levels Application Period*- Between September and November Application Process – Applicants can apply online through Minority Welfare Scholarships Portal.
